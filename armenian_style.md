# Armenian Subtitle Style Guide (Eastern Armenian)

This is the project house style. Follow it as written.

---

## Register and tone

Use standard Eastern Armenian (արևելահայերեն), literary register.

**Colloquial exception:** If the speaker's original English is clearly informal
(contractions, slang, fragments), mirror that informality — but avoid Armenian
slang that would feel unnatural in subtitles.

**Formality decision rule:**
- Formal English → գրական հայերեն, դուք where relevant
- Neutral/casual English → standard spoken register, դու
- Mixed → follow the dominant tone; flag in a note if genuinely ambiguous

Keep դու / դուք consistent per speaker throughout the episode. Do not switch
mid-conversation unless the source explicitly shifts.

---

## Translation approach

Translate meaning, not words:
- Reorder freely to fit natural Armenian syntax (verb placement, clitics).
- Keep idioms idiomatic — render the sense, not the literal phrase.
- Avoid calques; if a construction reads like translated English, revise it.

**Example:**
| English | Calque | Natural |
|---|---|---|
| "Make a decision" | «Կայացրե՛ք մի որոշում» | «Որոշե՛ք» |

---

## Line length — 42 characters hard limit

Every subtitle line must be **≤ 42 characters** including spaces and
punctuation.

When a line is too long, in order of preference:
1. **Compress** — find a shorter Armenian equivalent without loss of meaning.
2. **Split** — break across two lines at a natural syntactic boundary.
3. **Cut non-essential words** — remove filler or redundant qualifiers.

Never exceed 42 characters to preserve meaning that could have been compressed.

---

## Consistency

**Use the supplied glossary** for all recurring terms, names, and technical
vocabulary. If a term is missing from the glossary, translate it, note it, and
use that translation consistently for the rest of the file.

Proper nouns and brand names: keep the source form unless an established
Armenian equivalent exists (e.g. Երևան not Yerevan in Armenian text).

Numbers: spell out when the source spells out; use digits when the source uses
digits. Follow Armenian numeral conventions.

---

## Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| Over-translating removed filler | Adding «այո, բայց...» when source has none | Translate only what the English cue contains |
| Explanatory additions | Adding context the speaker didn't say | State only what is said |
| Agreement errors after reordering | Case/number mismatch after moving verb | Re-check agreement on every reordered line |
| Literal idioms | «Կոտրել մի ոտք» for "break a leg" | Render the intended meaning idiomatically |

---

## Handling uncertainty

- **Ambiguous register:** translate in standard register and add a translator's
  note flagging the ambiguity.
- **Line that cannot fit in 42 characters even after compression:** split across
  two cues; do not silently truncate or exceed the limit.
- **Unknown proper noun or term:** keep the source form, transliterate if
  needed, and flag for review.
- **Genuinely untranslatable phrase:** use the closest Armenian equivalent and
  add a note — do not invent a literal rendering.
