# Armenian Subtitle Style Guide (Eastern Armenian)

> Starter guidance. Replace or extend this with your own authoritative style
> guide — you know Armenian better than any default. RAG will use whatever is
> in this folder.

## Register and tone
- Use standard Eastern Armenian (արևելահայերեն), literary register, unless the
  source is clearly colloquial — then mirror the tone without slang overload.
- Match the speaker's level of formality. Keep the second person consistent
  (դու vs. դուք) within a speaker.

## Translation approach
- Translate meaning, not words. Avoid calques and word-by-word renderings that
  read awkwardly in Armenian.
- Reorder freely to fit natural Armenian syntax (verb placement, clitics).
- Prefer concise wording so cues fit within 42 characters per line.
- Keep idioms idiomatic: render the sense, not the literal phrase.

## Consistency
- Translate recurring terms the same way every time across all cues.
- Keep proper nouns, brand names, and technical terms consistent; transliterate
  only when there is an established Armenian form.
- Numbers: use Armenian conventions; spell out where the source spells out.

## Common pitfalls
- Do not over-translate filler that was already removed in the English step.
- Do not insert explanatory additions; subtitles state only what is said.
- Watch agreement (number/case) after reordering.
