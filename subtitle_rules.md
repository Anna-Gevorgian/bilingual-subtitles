# Subtitle Formatting Rules

These rules apply to both the English and Armenian subtitle tracks.

## Layout
- Maximum 2 lines per cue.
- Maximum 42 characters per line (including spaces).
- Prefer a single line when the text fits in 42 characters.
- When two lines are needed, balance their lengths; avoid one full line over
  one very short line.

## Line breaking
- Never break a word across lines.
- Break at natural syntactic boundaries. Prefer to break after punctuation
  (. , ; : ! ?) and between clauses.
- Do not break between an article/preposition and its noun, or between a
  number and its unit, if it can be avoided.
- Keep meaningful phrases together on the same line.

## Timing
- Cues must not overlap. Each cue starts after the previous one ends.
- Leave a small gap (about 2-3 frames) between consecutive cues.
- Minimum on-screen time is roughly 1 second; very short cues read as a flash.
- Maximum on-screen time is roughly 6-7 seconds for a single cue.

## Reading speed
- Keep reading speed at or below 21 characters per second (CPS).
- If a cue exceeds the CPS limit, it is too dense for its duration; either the
  duration is too short or the text should be shorter.

## Content fidelity
- Subtitles reflect what is actually said. Do not add information that is not
  in the source audio. Do not omit meaningful content.
- Remove only disfluencies and filler words; keep the speaker's meaning,
  names, numbers, and terminology intact.
